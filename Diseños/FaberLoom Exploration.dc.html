FaberLoom · Brand Identity

The exploration.

Three symbol directions and five wordmark faces for the parent marque — a loom of agents where the AI prepares the warp and the human weaves the weft. Low-fidelity first, then a recommendation. The colour palette is the only fixed element.

01

The symbol — three directions

Black-on-cream sketches. Treated as provocations, not a menu — each carries one line of intent and one line of risk.

A Woven abstract Recommended

Intent — Warp and weft interlace over and under as a plain-weave knot. No letterforms; reads as weaving itself.

Risk — Could flatten into a hashtag if strokes stay even; the over/under breaks and protruding thread-ends are what keep it a textile.

B FL monogram, threaded

Intent — F and L share a spine while a weft thread passes over and under the arms. Intimate, bookplate, an imprint.

Risk — Intricate — muddies below ~24px and can read as rungs before it reads as initials.

C Closed loop

Intent — One continuous stroke crossing itself — the human-in-the-loop drawn as a single process mark.

Risk — Loops are a crowded cliché; the making-and-weaving story quietly evaporates.

Recommendation

Direction A — the woven knot. It is the only mark that survives 16px, reads unambiguously as weaving rather than as initials or a generic loop, and carries no typographic era to date it. It animates honestly as a progress state — threads drawing on, over and under.
